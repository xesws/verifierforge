# v0.11.1 — Trainer: Blackwell smoke diagnosis and narrow fallbacks

The 30-step `grpo_v1_1p5b_blackwell_smoke` remains a single-GPU, 1.5B, `k=8`
probe over the frozen training pool. Before changing it, W2 records the
effective `trainer.n_gpus_per_node`, vLLM tensor parallel size, and GPU-memory
utilization from the resolved launch/config output, plus Ray placement state
and raw driver/engine log tails.

Classification is strict. A pending single-GPU placement group permits only a
matching resource declaration fix. An engine initialization hang permits
`enforce_eager` to bypass CUDA graph capture, then one attention-backend
fallback at a time, with every attempted/resolved option recorded. A
communication-init hang or any unclassified condition ends the run with
evidence; it does not invite speculative trainer changes.

W2’s total diagnosis/repair/retest budget is 45 minutes and it permits no more
than two retests. A passing retest must show actual rollout and post-update
evidence, a throughput value, append-only Storage metrics, a visible curve
point, and a clean W1 teardown. Only then may the pre-approved M3–M6 sequence
resume; the existing single-card scope and held-out-only claims remain intact.

**W2 diagnosis:** the active actor configuration, not the unrelated disabled
reward/teacher defaults, resolved to one GPU, TP 1, 0.5 GPU-memory utilization,
and `enforce_eager=False`. It loaded the model/FSDP and constructed vLLM, then
stopped before engine readiness, rollout, or an error. The first retest changes
only `actor_rollout_ref.rollout.enforce_eager=True`; it must remain TP 1,
single-GPU, and otherwise byte-for-byte equivalent in model/data/reward terms.
No attention-backend change is authorized unless that eager retest hangs.

**Eager retest result and final fallback:** `p0-w2-eager-r1-v0111` confirmed
the active eager override yet still stalled after WorkerDict initialization,
before vLLMHttpServer or a rollout. The final permitted attempt preserves
`enforce_eager=True` and forces vLLM's supported `TORCH_SDPA` backend via
`VLLM_ATTENTION_BACKEND` in both the trainer driver and Ray worker runtime
environment. No model-side attention implementation, TP, memory, dataset, or
reward setting changes in this fallback.

**Final W2 result:** the final `TORCH_SDPA` attempt received both the eager and
backend overrides but did not progress beyond Ray startup before the total
45-minute budget expired; it did not produce a WorkerDict, vLLM server,
rollout, metric, or checkpoint. The trainer is not admitted for M3/M4. No
third backend or trainer change is permitted without a new approved versioned
plan.
